Top 25 Waterfalls of Karnataka

Jog Falls

India’s second highest plunge waterfall located in Shivamogga district. The Sharavathi River drops from a height of 830 feet creating a breathtaking view. The monsoon season transforms it into a roaring spectacle. It is one of Karnataka’s most visited natural attractions.

Shivanasamudra Falls

One of India’s first hydroelectric power stations is located here. The twin falls Gaganachukki and Bharachukki are spectacular during monsoon. Situated on the Cauvery River, it offers stunning viewpoints. A perfect weekend getaway from Bengaluru.

Abbey Falls

Located in Coorg amidst coffee plantations and spice estates. The waterfall cascades down from 70 feet into a pool below. A hanging bridge provides a scenic viewing point. Best visited during rainy season for full flow.

Ishanya Falls

Hidden gem in the Western Ghats near Sakleshpur. A serene waterfall surrounded by lush greenery. Ideal for nature lovers and photographers.

Varahi Falls

Located in the dense forests of Western Ghats near Kundapura. A multi-tiered waterfall with a tranquil pool at the base. Perfect for a refreshing dip and nature walks.

Kalhatti Falls

Situated in the Chikmagalur district, it is a hidden treasure. The waterfall is surrounded by coffee plantations and lush greenery. A short trek through the forest leads to this serene spot.

Hebbe Falls

Located in the Kemmangundi hills, it is a two-tiered waterfall. The upper tier is called Dodda Hebbe (Big Falls) and the lower tier is Chikka Hebbe (Small Falls). The falls are surrounded by dense forests and are a popular trekking destination.

Unchalli Falls

Also known as Lushington Falls, it is located near Sirsi. The waterfall is formed by the Aghanashini River and drops from a height of 116 meters. The falls are surrounded by dense forests and are a popular spot for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Magod Falls

Located in the Uttara Kannada district, it is formed by the Bedti River. The waterfall drops from a height of 182 meters and is surrounded by lush greenery. It is a popular spot for trekking and nature walks.

Gokak Falls

Located in the Belagavi district, it is often referred to as the "Niagara of Karnataka". The waterfall is formed by the Ghataprabha River and drops from a height of 52 meters. The falls are surrounded by rocky cliffs and are a popular tourist destination.

Sathodi Falls

Located in the Uttara Kannada district, it is a hidden gem in the Western Ghats. The waterfall is formed by the Sonda River and drops from a height of 15 meters. The falls are surrounded by dense forests and are a popular spot for nature lovers and photographers.

Barkana Falls

Located in the Uttara Kannada district, it is one of the highest waterfalls in India. The waterfall is formed by the Seetha River and drops from a height of 259 meters. The falls are surrounded by dense forests and are a popular spot for trekking and nature walks.

Iruppu Falls

Located in the Nagarhole forest, it is a sacred waterfall for Hindus. The waterfall is formed by the Lakshmana Tirtha River and drops from a height of 170 feet. The falls are surrounded by dense forests and are a popular spot for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Bhadra Falls

Located in the Chikmagalur district, it is a beautiful waterfall surrounded by lush greenery. The waterfall is formed by the Bhadra River and drops from a height of 30 meters. The falls are a popular spot for trekking and nature walks.

Varahi Falls

Located in the dense forests of Western Ghats near Kundapura. The waterfall is formed by the Varahi River and drops from a height of 25 meters. The falls are surrounded by dense forests and are a popular spot for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Ishanya Falls

Kalhatti Falls

Jomlu Theertha Falls

Agaya Gangai Falls
